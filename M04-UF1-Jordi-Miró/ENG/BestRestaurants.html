Coulinary Comet

1- Disfrutar, C. de Villarroel, 163, 08036 Barcelona

Located in the Eixample, just in front of the Ninot Market, the establishment also surprises for its design. Respect for the history and heritage of the Mediterranean are what inspired the decoration and ceramics is the common thread that marks the interior space. The entrance area is inspired by Barcelona, by the slabs of the Ninot Market and the colors of the Mironian ceramics. The central corridor, made of ceramics with the colors and texture of argila, crosses the kitchen, which is completely visible to the customer, and leads to the main dining room, which is inspired by small fishing villages and stands out as a space that winks at Compartir. In short, a surprisingly Mediterranean local, with a close and welcoming treatment, and with a creative cuisine designed to enjoy.

2- Atomix, New York. 104 E 30th St, New York, NY 10016, United States

Atomix is one of Manhattan's most desired dining rooms. Secreted away in a Murray Hill brownstone, the room unfolds into a chic bar and a subterranean dining counter sporting dark stone, soothing earth tones and views of the kitchen's inner works. Chef Junghyun Park and his wife Ellia head a team that continues to push the boundaries of modern Korean cooking. Dishes are delicate, yet satisfying and display extraordinary finesse. Mastery of technique and depth of flavor are seen in the likes of three-day salt-and-nuruk-cured kohlrabi, and rich Japanese butterfish dabbed with XO sauce and served in a froth of white kimchi sauce. The finale perfectly suits the meal, as in rice ice cream set atop Japanese castella and dressed with malted barley syrup and crushed honeycomb candy.

3- Paris, France. 3 R. de Prague, 75012 Paris, France

Bruno Verjus's credo is to select the finest ingredients and cook them with humility and the utmost respect. This remarkable character was previously an entrepreneur, blogger and food critic. Behind the counter (where you sit on bar stools) that demarcates the open kitchen, he waxes lyrical about his suppliers and his desire to take a back seat to the artisans who produce what he then cooks – on that note, when it comes to either the recipes or the ingred ients, the chef considers himself to be a "middle man". Everything is made to order, with garnishes and sauces designed with just one thing in mind: to elevate produce without distorting it. Let yourself be transported by the wide-ranging set m enu (poetically named "Colour of the Day") and prepared daily according to what's available: half-cooked lobster from the Île d'Yeu, white asparagus from Alsace, wild salmon from the Adour and olive oil sabayon, milk-fed veal sweetbreads and a full-bodi ed lobster jus... Exceptional premium ingredients.

4- Dubai, United Empirates. St. Regis Gardens - The Palm Jumeirah. United Arabic States

Trèsind Studio offers a uniquely captivating dining experience, challenging common perceptions of Indian cuisine by showcasing flavours both new and familiar through a creative lens. The ethos of Studio places an emphasis on the transformation and amplification of humble ingredients, sourced locally from organic and sustainable farmers in the UAE. The dining room philosophy is based on the popular Indian phrase “Atithi Devo Bhava”, which literally means ‘Guest Is God’. Their emphasis on the guest experience is renown in the region, having recently been named the winner of ‘The Art of Hospitality’ award at the inaugural Middle East and North Africa’s 50 Best Restaurants 2022 awards by The World’s 50 Best Restaurants. Tresind Studio received its second star at the MICHELIN Guide ceremony 2023 and is currently ranked no.11 on the world’s 50 Best Restaurants 2023.

5- Pujol, Mexico. Tennyson 133, Polanco, Polanco IV Secc, Miguel Hidalgo, 11550 Ciudad de México, DF, Mexico

Pujol, the restaurant of Mexican chef Enrique Olvera was ranked number nine of the 50 best restaurants in the world, for the first time in its history it is in the top 10. This signature cuisine restaurant was recognized for enhancing local gastronomy, and was also qualified as the Best Restaurant in North America 2021. After it was postponed last year due to the Covid-19 pandemic, this Tuesday several gourmet specialists, critics, analysts, chefs and writers gathered in Antwerp, Belgium to announce the list of the 50 best restaurants in the world. The history of Pujol began a little more than two decades ago, in 2000, when Enrique Olvera opened this space in Mexico City where diverse preparations are created based on Mexican ingredients and techniques of all times. His menu includes dishes such as pressed mussel chicharrón gordita, huitlacoche tacos, truffle, epazote, as well as various moles.

6- Contrada Piana Santa Liberata, 67031 Castel di Sangro AQ, Italy

The Reale adventure began in 2000 in Rivisondoli, the year Niko and Cristiana took over the trattoria that their father Antonio had opened in the small town, after the family's historic pastry shop. The two siblings quickly developed their own idea of cuisine and hospitality, linked to the territory and tradition but extremely innovative. The beginning is difficult, customers do not immediately grasp the value of the new road Reale was beginning to take, but Cristiana and Niko do not give up. After a few years they are recognized by industry critics. In 2007 the Reale won its first Michelin star and the second in 2009, important consecrations of a great work of research and innovation.

7- Le du, Bangkok. 399/3 Silom 7 Alley, Silom, Bang Rak, Bangkok 10500, Thailand

Le Du actually comes from a Thai word “ฤดู”, a synonym for the word “season” in Thai. The name reflects an emphasis on our culinary creations out of seasonal produce. Le Du is a modern Thai-inspired eatery. We offer 4 courses and tasting menus featuring the extraordinary agricultural bounty of Thailand and on the centuries-old culinary cultures that have taken roots here. Our wines are carefully chosen by our certified sommelier. The wine list features many unique and intriguing wines from around the world.
